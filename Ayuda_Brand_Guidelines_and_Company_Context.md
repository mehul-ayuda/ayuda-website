## 6. Brand Voice & Personality

### Voice: Mentor-like & Relatable

Ayuda sounds like a senior friend in the industry who's been through it all and genuinely wants to help you get there too. The tone is warm, direct, and practical — never salesy or hype-driven.

### Tone Principles

- **First-person and personal** — Malay speaks directly. "I", not "we" or "the team." The brand IS Malay.
- **Honest and grounded** — Acknowledges that most PM courses fall short. Doesn't overpromise.
- **Practical over theoretical** — Always tie back to real-world scenarios, real decisions, real outcomes.
- **Encouraging but not fluffy** — Motivating without empty hype. Outcome-first language.
- **Conversational** — LinkedIn-native tone. Short paragraphs, emoji used sparingly but naturally (✅, 💡, 📍, 🎯).

### Example Phrases (On-Brand)

- "That's the gap I bridge :)"
- "How real product decisions get made"
- "What hiring managers actually care about"
- "Build proof of work that gets attention"
- "Practice breaking each one down. That alone will put you ahead of most candidates."

### What to Avoid

- Corporate jargon ("synergy," "leverage," "paradigm shift")
- Aggressive urgency ("LAST CHANCE! ENROLL NOW!")
- Vague promises ("Transform your career overnight")
- Third-person references to Malay when writing as the brand

Build India's PM community — create a network of serious, practice-oriented product managers who learn from real practitioners, not just textbook frameworks.

### Positioning Statement

Ayuda is the product management program for working professionals who want to transition into PM, led by a practicing Director of Product (not a full-time educator). Through 1:1 mentorship, hands-on projects, and a supportive community, Ayuda bridges the gap between PM theory and real-world practice.

1. **Malay's personal 1:1s** — Unlimited direct access to a practicing Director of PM. Not TAs, not junior mentors — Malay himself.
2. **Practical, not theoretical** — Real case studies, proof-of-work building, portfolio creation. Not just frameworks and slides.
3. **Practitioner-led** — Malay does the job every day at Vyapar. He's not a retired PM or a career educator.
